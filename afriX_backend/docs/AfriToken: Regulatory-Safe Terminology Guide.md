### When Discussing Fiat Integration

**The Challenge**: You need to explain that agents send/receive actual NGN/XOF.

**‚úÖ CORRECT:**
"Agents facilitate the exchange by receiving fiat currency (Naira or CFA) through their personal bank or mobile money accounts, then minting tokens to users. When users want to trade tokens for fiat, agents burn the tokens and send fiat from their personal accounts. AfriToken provides the escrow technology to secure these agent-facilitated exchanges."

**‚ùå INCORRECT:**
"AfriToken converts Naira to NT tokens and allows users to withdraw Naira from the platform."

### When Explaining Value Reference

**The Challenge**: NT has a 1:1 reference to NGN for user understanding.

**‚úÖ CORRECT:**
"NT tokens are designed with a reference rate of 1 NT ‚âà 1 NGN to make value easy to understand. However, NT is a digital token on the blockchain, not Nigerian Naira. The actual exchange rate when trading with agents may vary based on market conditions."

**‚ùå INCORRECT:**
"1 NT = 1 Naira. NT is backed by Nigerian Naira held in our reserves."

### When Describing Security

**‚úÖ CORRECT:**
"Your tokens are protected by blockchain smart contracts. When selling tokens to an agent, your tokens are locked in escrow until you confirm receipt of fiat. Agent security deposits ensure agents fulfill their obligations."

## üéØ Summary: The Golden Rule

**Always ask yourself**: "Would a regulator reading this think we're providing financial services or banking?"

**If yes, rewrite using**:

- Technology/platform language
- Peer-to-peer marketplace framing
- Agent as independent contractor
- Tokens as digital assets, not currency
- Facilitate/connect, never process/guarantee

**Remember**: Consistency is key. One slip into banking language in public communications could undermine your entire regulatory strategy.
